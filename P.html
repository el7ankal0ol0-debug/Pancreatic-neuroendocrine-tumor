PanNET Case Study

Definition

Pancreatic Neuroendocrine Tumors (PanNETs) are neoplasms that arise from the endocrine (islet) cells of the pancreas. Unlike the more common pancreatic adenocarcinoma, these grow slower but are still potentially malignant.

Common Factors

  • Genetic Syndromes: MEN1 (Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia type 1).
  • Family History: Increased risk with VHL or NF1 syndromes.
  • Hormonal Imbalance: Can be functional (secreting insulin/glucagon) or non-functional.

Pathology Pictures

+ Gross Appearance

Well-circumscribed, solid, yellow-to-tan mass. Often shows areas of hemorrhage or cystic changes in larger tumors.

+ Microscopic

Cells show a "Salt and Pepper" chromatin pattern. Arranged in nests, trabeculae, or glandular patterns with abundant granular cytoplasm.

Medical Case File

Initial Discovery: Incidentally found during a CT scan for kidney stones (2003).

Tumor Type: Insulinoma-like PanNET (Islet Cell Carcinoma).

Outcome: Liver metastasis led to a liver transplant in 2009.

Pathological Pearl: Immunohistochemistry (IHC) for Chromogranin A and Synaptophysin is essential for confirming neuroendocrine origin.
